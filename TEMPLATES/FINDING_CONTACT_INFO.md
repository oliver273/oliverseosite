## üéØ THE PROBLEM

**Common Issues:**

## üìß METHOD 1: FIND EMAIL ADDRESSES

### Option 1: Check Their Website (Thoroughly)

**Where to Look:**
1. **Contact Page:**
   - Most businesses have a "Contact" or "Contact Us" page
   - Check footer (often has email)
   - Check "About" page
   - Check header/navigation

2. **Footer:**
   - Many websites list email in footer
   - Look for "info@", "contact@", "hello@", etc.

3. **About Page:**
   - Sometimes lists owner/manager email
   - Check "Team" or "Staff" sections

4. **Check Source Code:**
   - Right-click ‚Üí "View Page Source"
   - Search for "@" symbol
   - Often finds hidden emails

---

### Option 2: Use Email Finding Tools (FREE)

**Free Tools:**
- **Hunter.io** (free tier: 25 searches/month)
- **FindThatLead** (free tier)
- **Email Finder** (various free tools)
- **Google Search:** "site:businessname.com email"

**How to Use:**
1. Go to tool
2. Enter business website
3. Tool finds email addresses
4. Verify email (some tools do this)

**Example:**
- Website: abcplumbing.com
- Tool finds: info@abcplumbing.com, contact@abcplumbing.com

---

### Option 3: Guess Common Email Formats

**Common Formats:**
- info@[businessname].com
- contact@[businessname].com
- hello@[businessname].com
- sales@[businessname].com
- admin@[businessname].com
- owner@[businessname].com
- [firstname]@[businessname].com
- [firstname].[lastname]@[businessname].com

**How to Use:**
1. Try common formats
2. Send test email (if it bounces, wrong email)
3. Or use email verification tool

**Example:**
- Business: ABC Plumbing
- Website: abcplumbing.com
- Try: info@abcplumbing.com, contact@abcplumbing.com, etc.

---

### Option 4: Google Search

**Search Queries:**
- "[Business Name] email"
- "[Business Name] contact"
- "site:[businessname].com email"
- "[Business Name] owner email"
- "[Business Name] [City] email"

**How to Use:**
1. Google search
2. Check results (often finds email on other sites)
3. Check Yelp, Google Business, etc.

---
