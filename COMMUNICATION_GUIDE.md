## üìû PHONE CALLS (For Complex Discussions)

### When to Use Calls:
- ‚úÖ **Discovery calls** (15-30 min) - Understanding needs
- ‚úÖ **Design approval** (if they prefer) - Reviewing mockups
- ‚úÖ **Complex questions** - Better explained verbally
- ‚úÖ **Issues/problems** - Faster resolution
- ‚úÖ **Final walkthrough** (optional) - Before launch

### When NOT to Use Calls:
- ‚ùå Simple questions (use email)
- ‚ùå Sending files (use email/Drive)
- ‚ùå Routine updates (use email)
- ‚ùå Payment/invoice (use email)

### Call Best Practices:
- **Schedule in Advance:** Don't cold call, set a time
- **Have Agenda:** Know what you'll discuss
- **Take Notes:** Write down key points
- **Follow Up:** Always send email summary after call
- **Time Limit:** Keep calls to 30 minutes max
- **Be Prepared:** Have their info, proposal, questions ready

### How to Schedule Calls:

**Option 1: Just Ask**
```
Hi [Name],

Would you be available for a quick 15-minute call tomorrow to discuss 
your website needs? I find it's easier to understand requirements 
over the phone.

Available times:
- Tomorrow 2pm-4pm
- Thursday 10am-12pm

Let me know what works for you!

Oliver
```

**Option 2: Calendly (If You Want to Scale)**
- Set up free Calendly account
- Share link: "Book a time that works for you: [link]"
- Automatically adds to calendar

### Call Scripts:

**Discovery Call:**
```
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time! I want to make sure I build 
you exactly what you need.

Let me ask you a few questions:
1. Tell me about your business - what do you do?
2. Who are your ideal customers?
3. What's the main goal for this website?
4. Do you have any content ready?
5. Any design preferences?
6. What's your timeline?

[Take notes]

Perfect! Based on this, I think the [Package] package would work 
great. Let me create a proposal and send it to you within 24 hours. 
Sound good?"
```

**Design Review Call:**
```
"Hi [Name], I've created the initial design for your website. I've 
sent you a link - can you see it?

[Wait for them to open]

What do you think? Do you like the overall look?

[Get feedback]

Great! I'll make those changes and send you the updated version 
tomorrow. Any other thoughts?"
```

**After Call - Always Send Summary:**
```
Hi [Name],

Great talking with you! Here's a summary of what we discussed:

‚úÖ Key Points:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

‚úÖ Next Steps:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]

‚úÖ Timeline:
- [Date] - [Milestone]

Let me know if I missed anything or if you have questions!

Thanks,
Oliver
```

## üí° COMMUNICATION TIPS

### 1. Always Be Professional
- Even in texts, stay professional
- Use proper grammar
- Be friendly but not too casual

### 2. Document Everything
- Important decisions in email
- Follow up calls with email summary
- Keep all communication records

### 3. Set Boundaries
- Business hours only (unless urgent)
- Response time expectations
- Preferred communication method

### 4. Be Proactive
- Send updates before they ask
- Anticipate questions
- Over-communicate rather than under

### 5. Make It Easy
- Clear subject lines
- Bullet points for lists
- One question per email (if possible)
- Include links, not attachments

### 6. Listen First
- Understand their needs
- Ask clarifying questions
- Repeat back what you heard

### 7. Be Honest
- If behind schedule, say so early
- If something's not possible, explain why
- If you made a mistake, own it

## üö® HANDLING DIFFICULT COMMUNICATIONS

### Client Doesn't Respond:
1. **Wait 2 days** - Send friendly follow-up
2. **Wait 5 days** - Send another follow-up
3. **Wait 1 week** - Final check-in, then move on

**Template:**
```
"Hi [Name], just checking in on [project/proposal]. Still interested? 
If you're not ready yet, no problem - just let me know!"
```

### Client Responds Slowly:
- **Set Expectations:** "I need your feedback by [date] to stay on schedule"
- **Give Deadlines:** "Please respond by Friday to launch Monday"
- **Be Understanding:** "I know you're busy, but..."

### Client Wants to Talk Too Much:
- **Set Boundaries:** "I'm happy to help, but let's schedule a call for complex questions"
- **Batch Questions:** "Let me answer all your questions in one email"
- **Use Email:** "Let's continue this via email so I have it documented"

### Client Is Unclear:
- **Ask Questions:** "Can you clarify what you mean by [thing]?"
- **Give Examples:** "Do you mean something like [example]?"
- **Repeat Back:** "So you want [thing], is that correct?"
